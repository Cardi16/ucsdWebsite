Love Live! Nijigasaki High School Idol Club

So, one thing I really like talking about for some reason is Love Live! and, of my favorite series in Love Live!, Nijigasaki High School Idol Club, especially. To give a brief introduction, Love Live is a series about girls that are idols, but in school, so they're called school idols (wow how original). For 3/5 of the groups in the series, their main goal is to get to and win the in-universe Love Live competition, for school idols, a very lofty goal. Along the way, the series is primarily known for its interpersonal relationships between the girls as they go on their journey to achieve the dreams, a la K-ON's 'cute girls doing cute things'. And, if you couldn't tell, this series is entirely made of girls, rather than boys in high school. (this will be a common theme)

The aforementioned Nijigasaki High School Idol Club, lined up on a rooftop.

Anyways, Niji girls are different because, unlike the 4/5 other groups in Love Live, they're not trying to get into Love Live. Their general idea is, rather than working as school idols in a group, their thing is that they're working as school idols, separately. General themes for them include: friends but rivals and even though we're working towards different goals, we're all friends and school idols together.

My favorite character in Nijigasaki and pretty much all of Love Live is Kanata Konoe, the sleep-deprived, hard-working girl that's amazing at cooking and also, for some reason, is everyone's therapist (headcanon).

D4DJ

Another series I absolutely adore is D4DJ, though I've not known it as long as I have Nijigasaki. D4DJ is a multimedia project about DJs, shockingly, specifically, DJ units of 4 people that perform together and their interpersonal and intrapersonal relationships. While that might sound literally insane---because how much content can you get out of being a DJ, really---but D4DJ barely manages to pull it off somehow. The characters are memorable, the rhythm game they have is fun as fuck, and, for some reason, I always keep crawling back to the literal crumbs of yuri they feed us*.
